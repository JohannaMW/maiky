„Schnitzler`s Dreams“ - an installative Opera

The performative Opera Installation „Schnitzler`s Dreams“ was performed at the newly renovated Aula at the library in Fredrikstad in August 2015.

Based on texts by 19th century writer Arthur Schnitzler and a collection of existing classical music, this work leads the audience into the depth of the unconsciousness. Similar to a hotel, this installation accommodates the different characters and the dreams worlds they carry within themselves. Like dream-walkers the characters move through the rooms. Their emotional state and their thoughts are revealed when they sing inner monologues extracted from texts by Arthur Schnitzler, accompanied by musicians who play newly composed music based on well-known pieces of classical music, mainly from the world of opera.

„Schnitzler‘s Dreams“ offers opera as a journey for the audience. A network of different stories develops. Deep hidden worlds and inner conflicts of the characters are revealed. The experience of the piece begins for the audience with their entrance in this dream universe and ends with their leaving. The audience enters this closed universe as a visitor. Each audience member can decide for themselves how they want to experience this spacial composition.

The world of dreams/ unconscious is the reality of this installation. Arthur Schnitzler was a writer and doctor to whom psychoanalysis played an important role. For 56 years he wrote down his dreams. These dream-notes, together with figures from other texts by him are used as inspiration. The characters and their inner conflicts are extracted from their stories. The aim was to find out if it is possible to create a waking dream in which the audience can move freely in a whole building will lots of staircases and rooms. This work explores where reality and fantasy can meet and how is it possible to keep the quality and character of fantasies within reality. With the help of music that escapes the intellect, and transports emotions directly, the result can be called an „installative Opera“.
